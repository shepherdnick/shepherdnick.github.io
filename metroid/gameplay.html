YOU ARE IN: METROID PRIME: GAME PLAY

Game play.  It's usually described as the way the player interacts with the character and how well the controls work.  If pressing a button on the controller makes the character react instantaneously, then the faster and quicker the game play.  Certain techniques are so advanced these days, game play elements alone can make or break a game.  Game play is not sensed by sight or sound, but is felt by the player.   Making the player think they are actually the character displayed on screen is a sign of good game play.

CONTROL

This game has excellent game play.  The player connects so well with the controller and the character, they feel like they are Samus inside an exoskeleton on Tallon IV.  The control mechanism was well researched, and the different buttons on the controller were assigned the functions because they were where the fingers fell naturally.  More information about the controls can be found on the control page.

SCANNING VISOR

One aspect of the game play and to keep with the theme of exploration in the game, there is a scanning visor which is available from the start.  The idea is to change to this every new area and every revisit to an area to see if there are objects to scan and download vital information.  There is a log book attached to the suit where you can look back and read over bits of information you may have missed.  Information can be saved about different creatures/ space pirates/ computer logs etc.  This adds to the game play feel and makes the player think they are solving all of the puzzles themselves.

MORPH-BALL

Like the games before it, the ability of Samus to change into a metallic ball (known as the Morph-Ball) means that exploration is made that bit harder and more exciting.  Attacks from fast moving enemies are avoided in the Morph-ball state, because a quick press of the boost button and the ball dashes out of the way.  The Morph-ball not only rolls about at a faster pace than the normal view, but it also has the ability to lay bombs in order to destroy crumbly walls, or annoying enemies.  The spider-ball also enables Samus to roll up walls and on ceilings via a spider ball track.

GRAPPLE HOOK

Unlike many other First Person Shooters, Metroid features a grappling hook (shot from the left arm not the right) which can be used to grab onto hooks. Samus is able to swing across dangerous caverns, and sharp drops without injuring herself.

DOOR OPENING

In order to progress in the game, certain doors have to be opened.  The method of opening doors in the game is to shoot them and wait for them to open.  The doors are uniquely hexagonally shaped, and have a certain colour around the frame.  This corresponds to a certain gun.  For example, a purple frame needs the purple gun (wave beam) to open it.  Other doors may require power to be restored to it before it can open, and in this case, a quick scan of the area will hopefully reveal a power source in need of reviving.  This can be supplied by the wave beam and another blast at the door opens the path back up!

SELECTABLE VISORS

Some creatures (mainly the Space Pirates) use optical camouflage and are invisible to the naked eye.  This is where the thermal visor comes in handy.  Everything that generates heat show up as reddy orange glows and this makes the Space Pirates easy to eliminate.  This is handy for heat generating foes, but for the type that do not produce any heat, another visor is needed. The X-Ray visor enables Samus to see enemies which are ghost like.  She can then easily defeat whoever dare attack.

PROTECTIVE SUITS

There are four suits available to help protect Samus against attacks.  The first is the normal suit which has a strong defense to enemy attack.  The next upgrade (VARIA SUIT) allows Samus to visit incredibly hot conditions, like in the Magmoor Mines.  The third suit  protects Samus in water filled areas.  This is a good feature of the game, but unfortunately, movement is limited therefore making combat harder.  This movement restriction is dampened when the GRAVITY SUIT is obtained.  The final suit (PHAZON SUIT) enables Samus to visit areas with high levels of radiation that would otherwise penetrate and harm her.  (The other suit is the Fusion suit).
