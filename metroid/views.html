Why I love Metroid Prime

I have always been interested in gaming, since I was very young, and I have grown up with lots of different eras of gaming.  I am also a fan of original games.  Even though there are other Metroid games, this is an entirely new perspective of how a game can be re configured.  Metroid Prime brings a fresh approach to gaming.

The way Metroid has created a new genre is very pleasing.  It shows that original game titles and good programming still exists and the effort and energy into making a good game is still out there.  One thing that did really impress me when I played it for the first time was the high levels of detail.  I'm not just talking about the character or the gun she's holding, I'm on about the environments and the story line.  The idea of scanning and downloading information is cool!  You can read back on some of the information you missed, and in some parts it's quite funny!  Not just one or two things in a room are scanned either - you can scan every enemy in the game - EVERY enemy has a background story! - amazing.  The graphical effects on the visor put a smile on my face.  When you look up into the rainfall, little drops of rain collect on the visor.  When you jump out of a pool, the water trickles off the visor!

I thought when I originally started playing Metroid Prime that the back-tracking would be very boring and tedious.  It actually proves quite fun - memorizing places that you can't get to because an upgrade is needed, like a spider-ball track, and then returning and exploring even more areas of the game.  The areas in themselves are massive.  Huge rooms and all linked by corridors.  Being levels themselves, they are just mind - boggling.

No speaking or movie sequences take place in the game to explain the story.  It makes you feel as though you are finding out the story yourself - very satisfying.

In all, I think this is one of the best games I have played for a long time, and will go down in history as a great.  I wouldn't have made this web site about it if I didn't think it was any good!

Nick Shepherd
