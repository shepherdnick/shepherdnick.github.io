The origins of Shepherdnick

Ever wondered where the name "Shepherdnick" came from?

Well, you'd have to be pretty stupid not to guess that it is my name only reversed. But why Shepherdnick?

Well it was back in the early periods of 1999 when I first got my old computer. After having the computer for about a month, I managed to pursuade my parents to get the internet installed on my computer.

Once online I was prompted to choose a username so that I could use MSN messenger and Yahoo! messenger online. I was stuck. No name what so ever in my head at the time.

So I did what I thought was best. I wrote my surname "Shepherd" and then added my first name to the end "nick". And it sounded quite good. For people that didn't know me, it would be quite an original name. And it stuck with me.

At first I was laughed at because people thought I was unoriginal, but I kept it, and the more I used it, the less it was linked to my actual name.

Since the beginning of the website, "Shepherdnick" is no longer a part of my name, it's a totally new noun all together!

The <SN> tag derives from a HTML tag, which usually begins and ends with the pointy brackets. I think it's quite a definitive tag, and anyone who see's it knows it's origins. Every good thing deserves its own logo, and that goes for SN too.
