What IS the fascination with Mobile Phones?

Can someone please tell me why mobile phones are so popular with the youth of today? And what makes them one of the most widely used pieces of portable electrical equipment available. It seems that this craze is only a nationwide phenomena, as only business-men and the like in other countries (e.g. USA) are the only people to use them.

I have a mobile phone. It's not the best on the market (depending on whatever you call the best) but it works. For me the only novelty is that you can use a phone anywhere in the country - anywhere meaning outside etc.

The newest of new mobiles can now surf the internet, check E-mail and play Games as well as sending text messages! But is that really what you want in a phone? Yeah, sure, text messaging can be fun for a while, but as soon as you realise you've spent 10 quid on sending squished up messages that are only viewable on a tiny screen, you begin to wonder about the fascination with it. Especially when you can use MSN messenger online, for free, and send many more characters at a time than that available on mobile phones!

ONE of the most annoying things in the world is when you are trying to get hold of someone via their mobile, and it just rings and rings and rings, or that the phone is switched off! If you've got a phone, answer it! But it's not always as simple as that - in some places its not convienient having them ringing all the time, because it interrupts things.

Another really annoying feature is that you have to pay to leave a voice mail message. You also have to pay to play it back. Why? I ask you.

Conclusion: Why bother having a multi-functional phone, when there are better alternatives available?

My mobile phone does its function incredibly well. The service offered is at a high standard, but at an extortionate price. The Nokia N-Gage however, is convinient, but the service offered is mediocre - What are you? A games console, or a telephone, or a PDA?

I use my phone in emergencies, or for convinience, when I can't find a payphone. I personally don't see the point of paying incredible amounts of money, when better alternatives are on offer.
