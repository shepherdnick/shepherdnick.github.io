The Future's Bland, The Future's P.C.

Today I was awakened once again by something that I think will be a very large way in which the future is shaped. By this I don't mean a future in which you won't be in. I mean within the next 20 years or so.

It was an announcement in my ICT class. It went something along the lines of "Pupils are no-longer allowed to use mobile phones with cameras to take pictures of either staff or fellow pupils without prior permission. This is illegal and the school will not tolerate anyone who does not comply with these rules. Severe clause such as banning of mobile phones on school premisses, or even expulsion".

It scares me to think that we are no-longer allowed to take photographs because of legal reasons. I ask myself the questions, Is it because people have a right to their own privacy, and their wishes must be respected? Is it because of paedophile laws, that we are no-longer allowed to take the photos? Is it because cameras take photos which can be copyrighted, just because of the people who are in it?

I'm not saying that these aren't bad, just that where does this put us for the future. If taking pictures of happy times in school of friends and teachers etc. is no longer allowed, then could this progress to the outside world. If we have to have permission to take a photograph of a person before we take a photo of them, could this progress into having permission to take photographs of other things, like landscapes, or inanimate objects? How can you resrict such things? Such expression of real feelings and life that photos portray. In the future will they have to be resricted to some sort of rules and regulations that makes sure they don't cause controversy or conflict?

I use my camera since having it at Christmas to take photos of friends in the common room and at break time in the hall, so that when I am older I can look back at the photo's I've taken and remember my time spent in school. I don't take photos because I've got some ill intent to modify the content of any photos and publish them for all to see. That I will agree is morally wrong. But that's not what I'm trying to complain about here.

What I have described is a basic example of how politically correct the world is. To be honest, you can talk about any type of apocalypse or absolution, but the way I think the way this world is going, is the way of political correctness to the extreme. In the future we may not even be able to voice our own opinion with a fear of the consequences. All emotions and feelings will be suppressed, and the world will turn completely neutral.

Maybe I've jumped the gun here and should explain myself better. Take an example, such as a P.C. terminology I heard the other day. Apparently travelling trades people, or gypsy's as I've always known them is now know as a racist term. Officially, if you want to be Pollitically Correct, they are called "Travellers". If you are seen saying gypsy then you are put down for it. Why? It's not as if you are accusing the people of being something they're not.

This could be extended into another way of thinking. For example, in the future, it could be wrong to say something like "he's a Norweigan", and instead it would be better to say "he is from Norway". This wouldn't be labelling him with anything, and keeps him happy, because you wouldn't sub-conciously be generalising him into the opinion you have of the people that come from Norway.

I saw a news article in a paper whilst on the train the other week. It was basically saying how in the future people will have to sign a contract before having sex. Two lovers, will have to sign a legally binding contract to say that they will have sexual intercourse. To me this would immediately take away the passion and naturality of the entire topic. Instead of being able to do something like that in the heat of the moment, they would have to sign for it beforehand. To me this is another example of how P.C. the world could get. What are the benefits? So the female couldn't call rape on the man, if they fell out after the relationship? So that the man would legally have responsibility for a child if it were born? Yes I can understand that, but it's not the natural human way to do things. It sounds more like what a computer would summarise.

If any of you have ever seen the film Equilibrium, you might have some idea of what I might be talking about. In the film, the "humans" have to inject themselves with a drug which suppresses their feelings and emotions. All the clothes they wear are bland lifeless colours, and their residences are of a minimalistic nature. Anyone who happens to collect objects which might make them happy, or sad, etc. and are found, are immediately sentenced to death by incineration. It is a very upsetting portrayal of the future, because I think in some ways it could possibly come true.

Of course it could all go the other way. Things may become so Politically Correct, that riots, and worldwide chaos could take place. Firefighters, Doctors, Police etc. cannot stop the rioting because they don't have any rights to touch other people without their permission, for example. It would be the ultimate democracy, ALL THE TIME. Everyone back answering and questioning each other, because they all disagree.

However, at this space in time, there is no real democracy. How can the government put a law on taking a photograph? What is a photograph - It's a collection of different light intensities and frequencies. A snap shot of time. A memory. If they can tell you whether you are allowed to own a memory or not, I don't think that is very democratic at all, do you?

So what am I trying to say here? Well, I am trying to say that, whatever way you want to look at it, the world is going to become more concious and stringent on what people can say/do/think. I don't yet know how it will effect us, but I know that in some way it will. Phrases like Bob the Structural Engineer and Pat the Postal Delivery Service Operator may be present.
