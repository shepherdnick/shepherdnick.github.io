Bude/|300|)/Bood/Bood Doods/Cornwall 2004 - Which ever way you want to say it... we went and this is my scrap book to remember it by!

Day 1 - 11/08/04

The morning of the 11th came very quickly, probably quicker for me because I've been working all summer. Time flies when you are having fun right? Well I wasn't so much having fun as boring my ass off, but still it came pretty quickly. I woke up really early to finish the last of my packing. You know - all the essentials - 1 litre of Bell's, hair wax, socks (?)...

Ready to leave the house by 7:30, Mum kindly offered to give me a lift to Dave's house where Lew had also slept the night. I managed to fit three huge bags, a tent a guitar, three 18 year old boys and my Mum into a Nissan Micra. Now that is a mean feat! We were the first to reach the gates of St. Albans at about 8:00, and I checked with this crazy looking guy sat in a minibus whether he was taking us to Bude... ("if you wanna go there mate" was his reply). Dave and I went up to Pontypool town to buy breakfast. I don't think there's anything more nutritious than 3 Nutri-Grain bars and a Share Size bottle of Coke.

By the time we got back to the mini-bus Ash and El had been dropped off by Wilf (love him - hairy ears!). Gradually more and more people turned up. It hit 8:30 and Ben was still no where to be seen. Typical - we all knew he'd be late. Still, he did turn up, slightly after 8:30. We piled on the bus, and I walked down the bus collecting the money. It was nice being able to hold £470+ in my hand at once. Smarmy git Cedric (we later found out the bus drivers name) took the £15 extra that was left over for himself, just assuming it was a tip! He could have used it to pay for the Toll bridge on the way back...

We stuck the second series of Fawlty Towers on the DVD player in the mini-bus and were on our way. We had lush weather, no traffic and made good time. We reached the camp site by about 12:30, and it had started to drizzle by this point. We bade farewell to oh-so-funny-money-stealing-Cedric who took our photo outside the Bus, and put up our tents a bit sharpish.

It was a good job we did too because we planned to walk into Bude just so everyone knew how far the walk was. Bad Idea No. 1. Torrential downpours hit us just as we set off and everyone was soaked through by the time we got to Bude. When we got into Bude, it had stopped raining. Damn you SOD AND YOUR STUPID LAW!!!

The first thing we all did when we got there was to visit the all famous REAL cornish pastie shop. Lamb and Mint all the way! It's just something about Cornwall, they make their pasties like no other place... same with the clotted cream... and the fudge! We then went to look around the shops in Bude. Ben and I scouted out the game shop (Game-Pad I think it was called) where we both pre-ordered our copies of Doom 3. The lady in the shop said that she would be getting them in the next day, and that we could come and collect them then. If you're not a gamer you wouldn't understand that getting a game a day before release day gives you a great feeling. Even though we couldn't play it. Ahh well. Go figure.

We then went up to Somerfield which was on the top of the hill. We stopped off in there for a few items like food, Pot Noodles or whatever, and somehow got split up. Ben, Plum and I tried calling the others who said they had gone to Crooklets beach, but were no-where to be found. We got fed up of looking and went back to the camp site, to be closely followed by the others who said they were in a cave sheltering from the rain. They did brighten up the camp by pulling out a Gazebo - the first team effort was put in to put it up and it was probably the best buy of the whole holiday. At least now there was somewhere we could all go and sit outside.

The weather was still really nasty for the rest of the evening, so we all cooked our Pot Noodles on the gas stoves provided by Kat, El and Jon, had a cuppa tea and then kinda did our own thing. A case of Budweiser was bought whilst we were in Bude and most of us had a few of them. It made the evening all the more stress-less. The 'lads tent' became known for having people in it in the nights because it was so big. The first night was ace - Dave, Lew, Kat, Ben, Tom, Jay, El, Plum and me were in there until very late singing songs like Bohemian Rhapsody... very loudly. The video is great - if you can be bothered to download it. I only recommend Broadbanders to do that. It's very big.

Choosing to sleep next to Plum seemed like a good idea after Spain. Not many complaints. My opinions changed on the first night. Imagine sleeping next to the loudest snorer in the world, times it by 10 and then you've got Plum. Kick him to make him turn over and shut up, and he gets louder. I was not impressed.

Day 2 - 12/08/03

A better start to the day was had by everyone. We woke up to a sunny horizon, even if the overhead clouds were black and uninviting. We got up, washed and dressed and had our breakfasts. The first morning, we did thank Tom for his imaginative and ingenious idea of bringing eggs on the trip. Good job they're not fragile!.... What? .... They're not?... Oh well. Jon cooked everyone fried egg and bacon sandwiches to fill their stomachs.

Nobody really had many plans for the day, but the horizon didn't turn into much. In fact it was pretty overcast for most of the day. We managed to get taxi's into Bude where we split up into our different groups. Ash, Lew, Jon, Kat and Dave went to the beach, and the rest of us decided to be healthy. No, we didn't eat a salad. No, we didn't go on a run. We went to the Splash - Calypso Health Suite. We got there and the queue was out the door, but that was only because there were so many people in the pool. The health suite was empty. £4 got us into the health suite for as long as we wanted, so we took the michael.

The health suite was just how I remembered it from two years ago - absolutely lush. It was incredibly relaxing considering the apparatus are designed to make you suffer. At one point we all went into the steam room and sauna together. Only the hardcore of us (i.e. me) stayed in the Sauna for the advised 15 minutes. Other than that we just chilled out in the jacuzzi. We must have spent about 3 hours in there before we got out.

When we did get out, we were right next to Safeways (the bigger of the two supermarkets in Bude). The weather had improved severely by this time so we phoned the others and decided to have a BBQ. I can't remember how much exactly we spent on food but it only came to about £2 each. We bought more booze, and ordered a taxi back to the camp site, where we met the others.

Lew, the BBQ king as he liked to be called, started cooking the food, and we all sat over on the picnic table banging our knives and forks on the table demanding the food. Well, not really. But we did chat one hell of a lot, and generally chilling out.

What a great evening! The sun was shining, we had good food, good drink and everyone was in fantastic moods. As the light got dimmer and dimmer, we all went over to the Gazebo, and sat around the ground mat. Someone had bought loads of those little candles for a £1, and we put them on a plate and lit them. It kinda gave a camp fire light and mood to the Gazebo. This was made all the better when Lew and Ben played classic acoustic songs we were all singing too. This one guy even came over to us from the tent opposite and said that his 5 year old son has just been put to bed, and could we all sing him a song to send him to sleep! (Seems cheesy I know, but it really did happen!)

Some went to bed, some came into the 'lads tent' and spent time talking... among other things... until 11 o'clock came and most of us went to Bed.

Day 3

The good weather from the night before seemed to want to stay with us, and without it being incredibly miserable, at least it was more bearable than the drizzle we had had before. The group decided that it was too good an opportunity to miss to go into Bude, so we called for more taxi's to take us in. By this time most people were already growing tired of each other, so most split off into their different groups. I went with Ben and Plum up to the shops around by the game shop. There was a little cafe we went into but everything was too expensive for the boys to pay for so we left.

We met back up with the others and decided to go down to Crooklet's Beach. By this time the weather had actually turned quite pleasant, and there were a surprisingly large number of people in the sea. Ashley and I did the best impulse buying in our lives - there was a market in the car park by Crooklets Beach where we bought the cheapest pair of shorts we could find (£5 I think?!)... and then we jumped in the sea. The surf was amazing, probably better because the tide was coming in, but the sea wasn't that cold surprisingly!

It did get quite cold after a while though, so we did get out. Not knowing what we were going to eat in the evening we all took a walk back up to Somerfield and bought tonnes of pasta and a pasta sauce. We also bought all the ingredients for making pancakes tomorrow morning. We got home and cooked the food, and piled into the lads tent. Some people chose to inhale some substances via cigarettes so the people that didn't went on a walk ... to the beach!

Widemouth bay was about a mile from the camp-site on a walk along a country road. The beach was incredible at night - it's a shame the moon wasn't out otherwise it would have been much better. The tide was out so we were able to walk right the way out to the shore.
