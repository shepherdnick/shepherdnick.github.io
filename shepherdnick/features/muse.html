Muse

One of my favourite bands - it has to be said. I was introduced to them by Ashley - only because he left their CD around my house this one time and I copied it.

The first album I heard - Origin of Symmetry - was unlike anything I had heard before. I mean, it was rock, but the lyrics and use of piano were awesome. The album blows your mind open with New Born - it's like a rollercoaster track, it builds you up to the top, all the time thinking its gonna get heavy any minute now, and before you know it, your racing through the song! Then you've got the slightly more odd tracks, where either the lyrics are not understandable (Micro-Cuts), or the music sounds wrong, but yet sounds right at the same time (Hyper Music).

Origin of Symmetry also saw the fantastic Muse cover of "Feeling Good". This song was featured on a coffee advert on T.V. for a while, but I had noticed recently it was changed with a different version of the song - and believe me, the change doesn't do the advert any justice.

Once the release of their new album - Absolution - was announced, I rushed to get my copy of it. I remember getting home from school, and playing it on the highest volume I could stand. It was like having a musical orgasm. The album is stunning. Whereas in the previous album the music did kind of fall away or seem empty in some parts, I thought that this album was completely full on with the music - and that includes the mellower tracks.

The concept of the album is about Absolution/The End Of The World - and boy does the message get across. The lyrics may seem pessimistic, but the powerful guitar chords and violent piano playing makes up the optimistic part. The album could have been very depressing, but its not. It does actually cheer me up to listen to, and I know this may sound morbid, but its how I think I would feel should I live through the apocalypse (whatever that may be).

After hearing the album, I went on to the internet and found they were playing in Cardiff - so I asked some people if they wanted to go see them with me, and to my surprise lots did. The very weekend after buying the tickets I went over my mates house and saw the "Muse Weekend" on Sky. They were showing the live "Hullabaloo" show they did. Out of many bands I have seen live footage of, Muse did actually entertain me whilst I watched. They are all musicians. Matt Bellamy plays lead guitar, keyboard and does the vocals, and he can change them during a song, and still make them sound great. I am so geared up to seeing them live, I might not make it until then without dying!

If you've heard the Origins of Symmetry album, then I suggest you get the new album. It's brilliant. (Although I have heard from other people that its not as good as Origins but each to their own).
