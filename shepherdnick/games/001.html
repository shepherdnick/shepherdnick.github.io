Boys with their Games....

The GameBoy counjours up ideas both good and bad by many people. Since its release in 1989, Nintendo have sold over 150 million versions of the GameBoy, making it the best selling games machine of all time. The recent release of the GameBoy Advance SP (Special Project) has made it the fastest selling console of all time.

The recent GameBoy Advance console was released in 2001 and claimed at being a 32-bit console (can roughly handle 32,000 colours), improved screen, an extra two shoulder buttons, longer battery life and was back-compatable with all the old games of the original console (a games library of over 1000 games).

It's only downfall was that by typical Nintendo style, they went for a non-offensive purple colour, which made more mature gamers turn their heads, claiming it was "a kid's thing". The other thing wrong was that the screen was slightly hard to look at. Most people who play them, struggle to see what they are playing because it relies on light reflecting of differently coloured pannels.

Not to be discouraged by the popularity (or lack of) of the GameBoy Advance with the mature gamer generation, Nintendo re-invented the design of the GameBoy Advance, and code named it SP (Special Project).

It was a sleek new lap-top designed machine, clearly with a more adult attitude as this advertisment shows:

The GameBoy Advance SP featured improvements on the original such as a backlit screen - for playability in the dark and better visiblity, a rechargable lithium ion battery (like in mobile phones) so that the GameBoy can be charged over and over again without the need of spending money on batteries. They also have metallic cases, which are more protective and make it look less like a toy.

Unfortunately, like all things, there was a problem, there was no-where to plug in your headphones. And the criticisms it got for that (and yet Nintendo have made an adapter available for anyone wanting to use the headphones). Personally, I could play GameBoy games without the midi-style sounds emitted from the little thing, as a pose to playing games on home consoles with cinemaesque stereo sound. Besides, look at all the other enhancements that Nintendo put effort into making it better - doesn't being able to see what you are playing feel better than only being able to hear it?

This console, now moving into the 21st century is setting new standards for portable gaming. Console designers have tried to over power it (look at the SEGA GameGear - it was roughly the same spec as the GBA now when the original GB was released) but the GB just kept getting better and better. Maybe it was Nintendo's reputation for making innovative and fun games that kept the popularity rising.

And what about the future of the GB?

New competition for the top portable gaming console is on the books for the GBA to put up with now as Sony plan to release their PSP (PlayStation Portable) at the end of next year. In my opinion, it will never do as well as the GBA - Nintendo have already got a huge fanbase with which to work with, and even though Sony also have a huge fanbase, releasing a new console onto a market which already has a dominant competitor is quite risky.

Well from the perspective that I have, the GameBoy franchise will continue on into history, as Nintendo seem to have it down to a fine art. The next GameBoy console will be an improvement on the current, and probably be bought by many a gamer. With most games being in 2D at the moment, maybe a move more into 3D would be on the cards? Who knows?! I don't know about you, but I'm looking forward to it!
