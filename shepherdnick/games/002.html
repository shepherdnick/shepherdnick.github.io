Anticipation of the greatest sequal... Half Life 2.

Why you ask I think that Half Life 2 could well be the greatest gaming sequal to be created? The fact that the first game is now being given away for free, having sold so many copies it doesn't need to make profit anymore is a pretty good starting argument.

I first heard of Half Life from my mate, and thought it was a good concept. You take part in a fatal scientific experiment releasing a portal to a hostile alien world, and enabling them to come to the Earth. Great! Another FPS! Don't care what the story is I just wanna kick some alien ass!! How wrong I was. Half Life is incredibly immersive. Never do you get the opportunity to see yourself (as Gordan Freeman), or hear yourself (GF) talk, making you feel as though you are actually Gordan Freeman.

Never was Half Life about the graphics. By today's standards the graphics suck. But you don't play Half Life for the graphics. The GamePlay and story were enough to surpass any bad feelings about graphics. The set pieces still bring a tear to my eye just thinking about them!

Imagine getting to an elevator door, and pressing the button to call it, only to see your ride hurtling down the elevator shaft filled with scientists, screaming to their doom.

The aliens themselves were immensely freaky. The dog that used to run around, with hundreds of eyes - but only one eyelid, and its way of hurting you was to emit pulses of highly pitched screaming. Very scary. A bash on the head with a crowbar would shut it up pretty quickly though.

Not only was the single player raising the bar for future FPS developers, but the multiplayer game Counterstrike was also kicking ass on the net. Even today, we still have the occasional match of CS on the home LAN my mate has. The new Countersrike seems to be more of the same just with better graphics.

So what about HL-2? Well the footage I've seen already about Half Life is unbelievable. The G-Man has muscles programmed into his face enabling him to pull facial expressions that look spookily real. Another aspect of the G-Man is that he has a sounds recognition, so he can talk in any language and look like he is pronouncing the actual words.

The physics engine is one of the best I have seen on any tech demo ever. The gravity of objects is phenomenal. In one scene, a line of enemies block your path, so what better to do than shoot down the crate hanging from a crane, and letting it swing into the enemies? I tell you what - by using the gravity gun, to pick up a nearby car and hurtle it into them.

Another impressive feature is the AI. In a scene I saw in a movie, the player went into a room, closed the door behind him, and dragged a cabinet behind it making the door unopenable. After realising this, the guard trying to get in, smashed the window next to the door and climbed in. How amazing is that?

Apart from the graphics being stunning and a definite improvement on the first game, the story is being kept a real big secret by Vivendi. All we know is that it takes place in "City 17" which seems to be a massive alien thing. When I know more, I'll update the site, but until then just enjoy the screenshots available from Planet Half-Life.
