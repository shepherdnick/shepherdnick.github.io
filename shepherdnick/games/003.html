SOUL CALIBUR 2 - The multi-formatting ass kicker

Well, that's how it is from my point of view. We play tested it last night and I was getting mixed responses. And I also saw some flaws in the game I was unable to see before. Well, I say flaws, but it wouldn't be the game it is without them. And they may not nessecarily be flaws to another person.

Most multi formatting games can be rather sucky at most aspects, graphics, gameplay etc. Look at Enter the Matrix - I played it and couldn't find something good about it. But a game that did sound out that was a multi-formatter was Burnout 2. Yes I know it's a racing game, but still, the game is fantastic on all formats! Handling of cars, fast, slick graphics, and a lush soundtrack. Then my opinion of multi formatters was changed. Some of them (only occasionally mind you) can be good.

This applies to Soul Calibur 2. I saw footage of it from the internet and the like, and it didn't look too bad at all. Then I had to consider which console to buy it for. Each format has got a unique character specific to that console. Nintendo's special character is Link from the Legend of Zelda. Being a recent fan of the Zelda series, I had to buy the GameCube version - just to play as Link.

Graphics - Let's talk about those. At first, internet movies didn't seem to show off the graphics too well, but after playing the game I realised they were very good indeed. Completely 3-D characters in a 3-D environment. The real time shadows are something of beauty, and go well on levels where there is a beautiful sunset. What's even more pleasing is that these superb graphics take less than a second to load on the GameCube version.

Sound - Like the first Soul Calibur game, the music is as epic and powerful as ever. With most of the game based in a kind of medievil atmosphere, the music goes well with the era. There is even a Hyrule stage, which has the Legend of Zelda music playing in the background. And another nice touch I liked, was that the music was very congratulatory once you had just defeated a character.

Gameplay - Now this is where it goes a bit weird. I'm not saying that the gameplay is bad, because I haven't found a problem with it, on the single player missions. The fact you can link combos to throw your opponent in the air, pelt them on their way down into the ground and then jump on them not only looks cool, but is satisfying when you pull it off. The problem comes with the multi-player aspect. This game just calls for button-bashers. If you wanna win - press all the buttons as fast as you can. I lost many games by being out of touch of this style of gameplay, and preferred kicking ass by knowing what moves I was inflicting.

Apart from this one aspect of the gameplay, it's a very good game. One which you can tell has had a lot of work put into it. Personally, I can tell Nintendo have put in work themselves for this launch - specifically with Link. If you have another beat-'em-up at home in your collection, then don't bother buying it - it is another arcade styley beat-'em-up. It's cool to have the weapons though.
