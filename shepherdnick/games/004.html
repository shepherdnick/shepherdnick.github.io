Halo - Politically Correct?

The time has come my friends, and for me - it ain't pretty. I very happily purchased my copy of Halo PC on Friday in the hope that all the problems of compatability with video cards would be over, and I would be able to enjoy playing a game that has given me great pleasure over the past year or so on the XBOX.

But, as most of the things that happen to my computer, it wasn't to be as simple as that. I had a feeling before I installed it that the Beta of the game might cause a problem with the full game.

Let me explain. The first thing I noticed once it had installed was that it requested I had the latest video card drivers installed. I haven't ever installed a game before requesting this, but wanting the best performance, I searched the nVidia web site and downloaded the latest drivers.

Tried it again. Hooray! Success - it started without a problem. Then I got to the loading screen for the first level expecting wonderfully bump-mapped, antialiased areas showing off the beauty and power of my computer. Nope. Wasn't going to happen. I checked the settings for the game - wondering why everything looked singularly textured. The settings were on an incredibly small resolution, the shadowing, specular and decals were greyed out so I couldn't even turn them on if I wanted, the refresh rate was set to 30 FPS, and the texture details were set on low. And changing them up didn't seem to do much.

Being quite annoyed at this time, I set about looking on the internet for Microsoft customer support on the matter. To my delight, there sitting on my screen I was one click away from a patch. I waited 4 minutes for its download, and installed, to be greeted by a scene of déja-vu. No graphical update was made.

Then I had another idea - maybe I could change the settings of the graphics card and see if that makes any difference. Got there, and the .dll file which made me see what I was changing, was corrupted, or didn't work, and closed its own dialog box.

So, apart from these ideas, Halo remains installed on my machine without the lushious graphics I was promised. I sit here and cry in wait for someone to reply to my post on the Microsoft discussion forums...

On the plus side, the processing allocation has been greatly improved - the game runs superbly well, although I fear this is due to the lack of having to process multiple textures and antialiasing.
