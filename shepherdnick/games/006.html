Mario Kart: DD!! (That's Double Dash people!)

Despite the forum debates, I went out and purchased Mario Kart: Double Dash!! I was looking forward to it. With the screen shots I had seen on the internet, I was going to be visually pleased, and probably not dissapointed with the handling of the game. I wasn't dissapointed.

Mario Kart, through the consoles, has proven to be a genre creating experience. It set the standards back on the SNES of how a Karting game should be made. My love for the original proved too strong when I bought the Mario Kart game for the GBA. I played it to death for a second time. But, unfortunately because I didn't have an N64, I had to wait to play the next Kart. Double Dash. And oh, what a wait! Now it's here and we's liking it :D

I got the game home on a cold and rainy day, slapped it in my machine and clicked the power on. The opening video came up and I was amazed at the visuals. They looked better on screen than any screenshot I had seen. I started (foolishly) on a 50cc cup, on Luigi Circuit. This was probably a bad idea of Nintendo to make this track available as the first track. To me, it did not impress. The environment was bland, with the detail only being on the characters themselves, the cars were incredibly slow, and the track was so simple a child-bearing giraffe wouldn't have had trouble playing it.

So after I won the trophy on 50cc, I immediately went on to 150cc and on the next cup. Oh. My. God. It was so fast I had trouble holding on to my toupé. I failed miserably. But I was eager to win. So instead I tried the medium speed, 100cc, and at least then I could compete. I was also pleased with the now stunning enviroments I was playing in. The DK Mountain track is awesome. It has a cannon at the bottom of the mountain, which shoots you to the top, whilst the track spirals away underneath you. I liked the Mushroom Bridge track too, you race on roads with other vehicles on it, but you've got to make sure you don't bump into them, or you go kicking.

The more and more I played this game, the more and more I loved it. The controls just felt right, the way the game mechanics worked, the way the AI reacted. It feels so much like the old Mario Kart. But I don't yet know whether that's a good thing.

So here's my verdict on the game

Graphics: Very sweet. I've yet to see a jaggy. And 2D trees? Not in this game friend. Probably the only racer in the world without 2D trees. The character design is impecable. I have to say they are the best graphical feature I've seen on the Mario Series. Just one point I'd like to note: the Mario Kart Series has always followed the graphics of the Mario Game before it, so with this game, it follows Mario Sunshine.

Sound: Lacking slightly. Not like the original Mario Kart where you could tell what track it was just by hearing the tune. This game doesn't have a stunning soundtrack. I usually switch on some other music whilst I play. The sound effects however are brilliant! It makes the game that much more fun hearing your opponent cry...

Gameplay: I think the turn of phrase ("How do you turn a phrase?") is, "It plays like a b!tch". Once you are playing, you just relax and the controls come to you. I think that's what makes it so yummy to play.

Multiplayer: My mate and I spent about 3 hours solid playing the multiplayer features of the game. The best bit about it is the co-op mode they've got on there. The game has been made so that certain elements require both players to interact in order to pull it off. Like the sliding boost, the driver initiates it, and then the weapon player has to wiggle their analogue stick in order to get the boost. If you don't own any multiplayer games, and want to: Get this! Its great!

Conclusion: Fantastic game, really fun to play, and multiplayer is cool as hell. But in my honest opinion, if you've played the original to death, it's too much like that to worth worrying about. If you want a breath of fresh air, buy F-Zero, it's a different style of gameplay.
