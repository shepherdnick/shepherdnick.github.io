A Film Noir Love Story: The Fall Of Max Payne

I have lots of good things to say about Max Payne 2. Mainly that it is one of the best gaming sequals I have played in a long long time. The story itself is worth playing the game for. The game would make an awesome film. I love the fact that the graphic novels help tell the story as much as in game videos. Max Payne 1 was an innovation in gaming when it was released, by including the new "Bullet-time" mode where you could slow down time, just like in "The Matrix". People were stunned at the effect, and the game sold millions. To this day there still remains speculation as to who invented the "Bullet-time" effect.

Following on from the climax of the last game, it cleverly shows you near enough the end at the beginning, then spends the rest of the game explaining how he got there. The basic story is, that he needs to finish his own case of who murdered his family. This story is coupled with the fact he is in love with another woman, Mona, who is also on the run from the NYPD. Max finds his old aquaintance, Vladamir. Then the story moves on...

Well, I had this game during half term. I completed it within two days. They seem to have made this game a lot easier than the first. Or maybe I'm just too God-damn good! Any ways, if you want the game to continue the story, then go ahead. This game has got harder difficulty settings, which are unlocked when you complete the game for the first time, and the difficulty does get extremely hard.

Graphics wise, the game doesn't push the polygons, but contains a hell of a lot of beautiful textures. They must have been made by an artist or something because they look real. Honestly. You would not believe how good the game looks, and runs so smoothly as well!

The sound has been polished off as well. The narrativeness mimics the first, with some great (and toungue-in-cheek) metaphors, that make the game great. The soundtrack is top notch. A band called "Poets of the fall" wrote the theme song, and the new cello based theme tune is very powerful.

Gameplay: just like the original game. Pinpoint accuracy when in bullet time mode, and as always, cool side jumps etc. make you look like "the man" when you're playing.

The Havoc ragdoll engine is so fun to play with! You can get fantastic positions with the dead bodies you create. Unfortunately though, the dead bodies have no damage engines, so they don't fall apart after 20 minutes of shooting with a shotgun (what? no I didn't do that.... honest....).

Final verdict: Fecking cool. Get this game if you have a good PC, and play it to death. You will enjoy it, trust me.
