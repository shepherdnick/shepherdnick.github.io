First Person Non Heros

I was thinking the other day about different kinds of games. Mainly the likes of Max Payne, Metal Gear Solid, Halo, and Half Life. The difference you ask me? The first two are third person, and the last two are first person. But they come under the same kind of catergory, and that is a weapons based game.

And what was I thinking about these games? Well, Metal Gear Solid is my all time favourite game. Max Payne is closely behind it. Solid Snake is my gaming Hero. Max Payne is also Uber Cool. As is Gordon Freeman and Master Chief.

But when I got to thinking of it, with as much respect to Master Chief, Gordon Freeman, and many other First Person Shooter characters, how could they be someone's hero?

When you play a third person game such as Max Payne, or Metal Gear, you can see the character on the screen. You interact, and can see their movements. Whereas, when you play a first person game, you ARE the person, and there doesn't seem to be much interaction. You are basically simulating your movements in a virtual world. There is too much of a sense of realism for the player to feel attached to the character they're playing as.

But with the likes of Solid Snake, and Max Payne, the player can constantly see who they are playing as, and probably more importantly, the types of moves they are making. Another way of looking at it is, the third person perspectives give a constant in-game video of what is happening, making it more like an interactive film.

On the other hand, the more FMV's or in-game videos there are in the game which involve the character to talk or take some sort of action, gives the character more of a character. Gordon Freeman for example, doesn't even talk in Half-Life and the player cannot relate to him in any way. At least in Halo we see the Master Chief talking to Courtana.

Oh well, I don't know whether you could understand what I was trying to say there, but if you could, do you agree? Who is your gaming hero, and are they portrayed in a third person, or first person perspective? What is it about your hero that makes them special? Just something to think about.
