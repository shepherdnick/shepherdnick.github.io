I hate RPG's

Always have. Always will. With the exception of Pokémon Blue/Red, but everyone loved that.

I have heard from people that Zelda: The Wind Waker was also an RPG, and at first I disagreed as it didn't follow the sterotypicalised fashion many RPG's take on. Then I got to thinking, wait a sec, I loved that game, and I don't really like RPG's. And then I got to thinking if I really did like the Zelda game, really, deep down, is it one of my all time favourite games? The answer is No. But, I still disrepute the fact that it is an RPG, Shigeru Miyamoto, the creator of this Zelda game and many a Nintendo classic, has the same opinion as me of RPG's, he hates them.

Zelda doesn't fall into the category of what I think is a classic RPG. And that is, you play as a character who goes on an epic journey though an uncharted land in search of a mystic object which will stop the destruction of the land, and/or bring your enemy to his knees. Generally lasting more waking hours than is neccesary, these boring tales can last weeks upon weeks of training in the barren land where you come accross random weak foes in order to gain a few statistics to add to your Hit Points in order to defeat the next boss without dying. This coupled with the fact that rarely does a game of this stature have impecable graphics (with the exception of Fable on the XBox, that looks stunning, graphics wise) or vivid gameplay, more like a collection of poorly playable games which wouldn't be enough to publish as a game themselves, and the database of three lines every character has to say.

Statistics, Statistics, Statistics. Hit points, Power points, Attacking points, Speed points, Defensive points, Level points, Experience points and many many many more numbers are usually a huge part in RPG's. As much of thrill I get about numbers, theres only so many times I can scream with delight at them. Mainly when they've got £ signs before them. No offence guys who like these games, but if I wanted to look at statistics, I would have chosen to work for the Guiness Book Of Records or something.

A more interesting game for me, would be maybe an action/adventure game where you have to kill enemies in real time, and occasionally talk to relevant characters. The story would portray just as well, and the action would be more constant. Now that sounds like fun. Ever noticed you can play an RPG by either hitting the same button over and over again, or play one handed ;).

Wow. I've bored myself. That's gotta be a first. I'm going before anyone realises I'm still talking. You still reading? Oh. Your asleep.
