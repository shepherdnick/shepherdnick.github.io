XIII

Well it took less than a month to complete and that wasn't even playing it solidly. I don't have a hell of a lot of time to play games much these days. But still, that's not to take the shine off of this game. Cel-shaded may be making a come-back especially being more popular since the release of Zelda the Wind Waker and the up-coming Killer7 and this game is no exception to that genre, but don't let it put you off.

If you want a bit of style and an imaginative new gameplay elements (despite the fact it runs on the Unreal engine as UbiSoft don't own their own FPS engine) then this is a great game. Never before has a cel-shaded game look so fun! I remember seeing this game after E3 last year, and when I heard it was going to be multi-format I was not looking forward to playing it. But then one day last year I managed to play on the demo over my mate's house and was surprised at how challenging yet easy to control it was...

The game story I would have said was cliché but after thinking about it, I can't remember any other game where you are accused of shooting the president of the US. Or when you wake up on a beach and have the number XIII tatooed on your chest with no memory as to how you got there. The story continues ... you find someone to help you find out your real identity again after the government conspires that you were the 13th member of a secret society of terrorists (who look like the Klu Klux Klan!) that had plotted to assasinate the president. And you have to prove your innocence.

It is a really cool story, and without releasing too much information, the end levels are awesome. But more about the important features now...

Graphics: Dated because of their cel-shaded style, yet that's also what makes them modern. I've never seen such stylish graphics. If you've ever wanted to play a game around a comic book style theme, this is the game for you. It even pops up with three little cells in the corner of the screen when you get a head shot. The comic book appearance continues with the old-style large font words that come up on the screen to depict a noise, like when the guards are patrolling the words "Tap" appear at their feet. When you launch a rocket, the screen is filled with "Baooom!" The FMV movies are stunning. They are in a typical comic book style, only moving. Fun ^^

Sound: Surprisingly, very very good. Compared to other game sound tracks of recent times, this is fantastic. Music of suspense and alert are all dynamic so that the speakers are never silent. The voice acting is not too over the top either. Pretty normal for an American comic style game. I wouldn't reccommend the game for the soundtrack, but it is certainly one of the game's better points.

Gameplay: Well, what do you want me to say? It's the Unreal Engine. Anyone who's anyone has played a ggame with the Unreal engine, so this game is very good. The only real drag is the menu system, although it's customisable. Other than that, the camera moves well and the movement controls are tuned to perfection.

Final Verdict: A bit samey in some levels, but the occasional stealth levels are fantastic. The graphics give a unique feel to the game, and it's generally a fun game to play.
