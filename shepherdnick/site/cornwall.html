*UPDATE 29/07/04*

SO NEAR, YET SO FAR...

The beach is so close you can almost smell it huh? With 14 days left on the clock, I think I should round up all that is going to be happening. But first, I think some things need to be cleared up.

  1. We will be staying on a Family Camp Site. This does not mean an 18-30's drink all you can in the space of 15 minutes and makes lots of noise to the early hours of the morning. It means we have to take other peoples situation into account and be aware of our surroundings. Loud noise is a No No into the early hours of the morning. I am not saying this only for the benefit of the people around us, but also for our own. We don't want to be getting chucked out of the camp site any earlier than the 18th of August, yeah?
  2. We are going to be camping in Britain. As we all know, we aren't experiencing the best of weather this summer, and there could possibly be the slight chance we will be experiencing rain. Last time we went, we were lucky that it didn't rain the whole time we were there, but weather is a fickle thing. There are not that many indoor activities in Bude, however there are so many of us, I am sure we will be able to think of something. Just be aware of it, for the sake of clothes.
  3. Facilities available on the camp site include: Hot Water in the Shower and Toilet blocks which also include hand basins; washing up bowls for dirty dishes and general cleaning; public telephone; facility to charge mobile phones; designated barbeque areas; swimming pool; on-site bar; on-site shop. Facilities that are not available and I think you should be aware of: There are no electrical power points that are made available to us, so don't bring anything electrical (torches will have to be our main source of light at night); there is no on-site restaurant or cafe, so if we want ready made food we will have to go into Bude; Barbeques are not allowed on the plots where the tents are pitched and can only be used at the designated areas; those of you taking food, there is no way we can keep the food cold, as there is no fridge available (we bought milk last year, but had to make sure we used it otherwise it would go off).

WHEN?

Well considering I'm going on holiday with the other guys on the 29th June for a week, and other people's commitments, then we decided that we should probably go to Cornwall in August. The date for this was Monday the 16th August for about a week. I think that's what we estimated wasn't it?

WHERE?

Good question. Jon, Kat and I were in the cafe earlier on this week and there was some confusion as to what we all wanted out of the holiday. Two years ago, Tom, Robyn, Jon and myself went on holiday to Bude in North Cornwall. You can see the pictures of our time there here. We had a wail of a time, and considering it isn't exactly built for people our age, we did find plenty of things to do.

In the cafe, Jon said that he wouldn't mind doing the same sort of thing again. And to be honest, neither would I, although I am biased. I lived there for a year! Kat said that she is likely to get bored there without plenty of things to do. I can understand that side as well though, if you want to go on holiday, you just as well go to do things rather than laze about all the time.

So what we need to decide people is where exactly we want to go. If we choose to go to Bude then there are things to do, and quite a few places to visit, but like many other things, they all cost money, and may not be to everyone's cup of tea. I got lots of information from http://www.visitbude.info

Newquay was another idea for a place to go. It's the surfing capitol of Britain, so there's kind of a lot of things going on there. It's also a lot bigger than bude (from what I can remember). Lots of the info you may need is here: http://www.newquay.co.uk/

I did also suggest (technically not in Cornwall) Torquay (home of Fawlty Towers don't ya know! It has a massive water park that I can rememeber. It was pretty fun there too, and it was a bigger town than Newquay, so there is plenty to do if you get bored. Plenty of places to eat and that. http://www.torquay.com/

Anyone else got any suggestions, then please tell me, and I will relay the information onto everyone via this page.

WHAT'S THE DILEMMA NOW?

Well, first, can you afford to go? Please remember that £60 is only a rough guesstimate as to how much you want to spend on food. This is a figure that I would think about taking because I know I would be on the safe side. Don't forget we are going on holiday to a holiday resort where the price of things generally tend to be more expensive than they are here. Do you have the potential of getting £143.97 by that time. What about getting the £47.43 (standard price) by the proposed Friday the 28th May?

Second, do you want to miss possibly the most important day of the year? The exam results are out on the 19th of August, and if we travel bvia National Express, we will HAVE to miss it.

Third, if we don't go via the National Express, then is the alternative to rely on people to drive? If people are driving we will need at least 2 cars that can seat 5 people. We may even need more due to luggage etc. How do the people who are driving feel about driving on the Motorway or for that amount of time? It's a hell of a drive, looking at about 4 to 5 hours. Maybe even 6 if you get stuck behind a caravan.

Fourth, I know the music fanatics of you are interested as to what date the Brecon Jazz Fest is: Fri the 13th of August to Sun 15th August. The Bude Jazz fest is on: Sat 28th August to Sat 4th August.

Just some food for thought. Discuss.
