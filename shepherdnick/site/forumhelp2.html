Shepherdnick's Forum Image Guide

In light of people wanting to post their images of stuff on the forum, I have kindly made another step-by-step guide for you to do it properly, and I will try and explain how you can keep good netiquette.

  1. First you need to find the location of the file on the internet. You can't use local files from your computers hard drive unless they are on the internet. You will need to find the properties of the image thats in the web page. To do this, right click the image, and click properties. Up pops the properties dialog box. Amongst other things, it tells you the URL of the image. You can highlight this URL. I suggest you do just that and then press Ctrl and C to copy the URL to your computers clipboard.

  2. Then you need to open the image tag. It goes a little something like this (check this out) <img src=" - and then all you do, is put your cursor next to the " and press Ctrl and V to paste the URL next to the ". Now it should be looking something like this:

    <img src="http://www.shepherdnick.co.uk/images/shepherdnickcouksplash.jpg

    *By the way, the "img" and "src" are abbreviations of the words "image" and "source" respectively*

  3. Then you need to close the tag. Simply close the quotation marks, and the brackets, "> - like that. Cool ain't it? The final image code should look like this:

    <img src="http://www.shepherdnick.co.uk/images/shepherdnickcouksplash.jpg">

    And the result gives you:

Right then - here comes the very important part of this whole here web page.

In keeping with netiquette and the internet code (I know, I know, there's nothing written down that says you have to abide by these rules or that there is a written internet code!) it is advisable that you don't sponge images.

Sponging images basically means you are linking to an image that is not held on the servers of the site you are linking from. This is bad because you are in effect stealing bandwidth (the ability to transfer data, of which there is a limited supply) of the server where the image is held. The image you have linked is being downloaded by everyone viewing that particular page, but using the processing power of the server where it is held, and could possibly inhibit someone trying to download the file from the original web site.

It is good netiquette to ask before you save images from certain web sites, or indeed even sponging from them. Some people allow it, others do not (and will let you know about it!) - and others don't even allow you to get the address of the image at all by disabling the right click. (As a web designer, I know how to get around this however!).

So just remember - sponge if you can, but think of the consequences first! Always ask before you save and re-distribute images from web sites! They are all copyrighted!

Hope this helps you guys!
