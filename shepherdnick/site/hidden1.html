The explanation

OK, I'm going to start typing, and see what happens. I'm gonna try and sort out in my head why I'm feeling like I'm feeling, and at the same time hopefully answer your questions about why I may come across as being down.

Stress. Any one want some? I've got plenty to share. I may not feel or look as though I've got stress, but it's hard to explain. It's not a school work overload of stress, school work is fine. It's a relationship stress. A relationship between my friends, with my family and between people who aren't me. Why am I stressed relationship wise? God knows. It's probably due to the amount of relationships I am loosing at high speed. I used to have so many acquaintances. Now they seem to have fizzled up and died. People who I used to get along fine with, I don't even speak to any more. Close friends whom I considered loyal, and reliable, have up and left me. Though, moving to St. Albans did start off new friendships, like the one I have with Ben, and the one I have with Lewis. I guess what they say is true, you're school days are your best.

Maybe I liked West Mon a bit too much. I made lots of friends there. I grew up with most of my current friends there. West Mon was a fantastic place. I studied the subjects which interested me, and with the friends and teachers I knew well. I am a sucker for traditionalism, and I work well to a routine. West Mon had a routine. I knew where I had to be, and what time I had to be there. I knew what would happen if I mis behaved, and I knew what would happen if I made a good piece of work. I knew where people would be and what they would be doing at break time and at lunch time. I had a good idea of what I would be doing in the evenings and what I would do on the weekend. It is this routine that I now lack in my life that has probably made me feel down. The only routine I have in my life these days is the mornings, where I know that I have to be in school for registration period, because I know lots of my mates will be there too. Other than that, the day is my own, as is everybody else's. How can I rely on meeting people in the day, if they do the same as me and bugger off home at the nearest opportunity. There's no community with the sixth form like there was in West Mon.

And what is the cause of this relationship with friends to decrease? The sudden realisation that other girls/boys exist. I'm not blaming people to want to spend every waking moment with each other. In fact, I like the fact that most of my friends have got other halves. What I don't like is the fact it gets rubbed in my face every which way I turn. I can start talking to some one, or flirting at least with some one, and then all of a sudden remarks such as "Where's *, I'm bored of you now?" or "I haven't seen * all the time I've been talking to you", kinda make me feel worthless and I wonder what was the point me talking to them any way. Then when the other half does turn up, why do I bother existing at all? All they want is to be with each other, and therefore I am irrelevant. Another thing that annoys me is the petty arguments and jeleousness you all have about each other. Get over yourselves. You are all just turning into adults, the relationships are in no way serious, you're not going to marry the person you're going out with, so just ENJOY your time with them while it lasts. For God's sake it's more than I've got.

I myself long for a relationship. I don't want sex. Hell, I could live without kissing. I'm not ready for it. I just want to feel loved. I want to hug someone, and for them to hug back with feelings that make me feel wanted. I would love to be relied on by someone. As I am given more and more independence, this makes me feel all the worse. I used to think I needed a partner to show off like the status symbol they appear to have grown into these days around school, but not now. I know that I don't need a partner to show off. I need a partner because I need to feel useful, wanted and loved.

To add to the fact that friends have left me, or don't talk any more, also means my social life has taken a nose dive. I used to like playing video games. Not any more. It used to be my hobby, but now I think - what's the point? Who can I possibly talk to about computer games. They are a source of entertainment, no different than watching a film or listening to music, and show no possible creativity that is missing from my life. I can't call it a hobby, because no skill is involved, and anyone can play a game if they've got enough money. My mates don't agree with everything game related either and most conversations/debates on the topics end in tears. So I choose not to talk about them.

But then what do I have as a hobby? I could call having HTML as a hobby, or owning my own web site, but that's not really a hobby. Yeah, I've got a interest in it, and when I can be bothered I can write some, but other than that, it's peoples job - people make these web sites for money. I'm in denial as well, yeah I do use Dreamweaver. I know it's not as versatile as HTML allows it to be, but I use Dreamweaver for convenience. The template facility helps me greatly with my web site. (Just to clear up, I wrote the code myself, seen as Dreamweaver would find it hard coping with the buttons and tables etc. I just use it for the templating and cache facility). So really that's another one of the things I'm liking about my life - I have no culture - nothing I am specifically good at that I can talk to other people about. What's the phrase? "Jack of all trades, Master of none".

What do I do other than HTML? The answer is not a lot. I sit on my arse at home at the computer surfing the Internet. Not even spending time with my family. I look at silly non-informative web sites, or continually checking web sites for statistic etc. instead of doing school work. Yeah, that's another thing I want to get off my chest that I'm in denial about. I don't do school work, or as much as you like to think I do. I doss around doing sod all with my time. Occasionally I might do some school work, but I hide behind the fact that I have school work to do in order to tell my mates that I don't want to go out socialising with them. And I don't want to go out socialising with them because my parents have said that I go out too much and don't spend enough time studying.

A couple of weeks ago I wanted to drop Geography. One of the subjects that I chose to take for an A level. I wanted to drop it because I am finding it hard doing the work for the courses that I'm doing. It would free up a lot of time, and help me to maybe get a better set of grades if I didn't have to worry about them. But then after talking about it with my parents, they told me that I wasn't working hard enough at my school work and never seem to do work in my bedroom, I just seem to be messing about with my computer. They are not wrong. That's all I seem to do. So Yes, I probably am not working hard enough, and Yes I have wasted the last year of my life. Now I'm worried that I've left it too late to catch up any missed work and class notes that I haven't already caught up on. Does this mean my grades will drop next time I do exams, therefore not getting me the grades I want and confirming what they said earlier when I will be disappointed with my grades.

My mum recently (5 years ago) took an AS level in accounts and spent most of the year from Christmas to May in her bedroom revising for it. I do four subjects. I should be doing four times the amount of work that she did, but I don't. That makes me feel guilty. But I then have to ask myself why I feel guilty. And the fact is, that my mates are happy at home or out with their other half, who is also happy, and they don't mind doing their school work, because they've got someone there who will help them and give them an incentive to do the work. It doesn't matter to them if they can't/haven't done the work, because someone is there to care about them. What incentive am I given? A thought that if I don't pull my weight, I won't be able to go to University, I will be disappointed if I get bad grades, and it's the end of the world if I don't study.

Then do I really want to go to University at all? My parents seem to not want to help me, by always giving indications of how bad it is. For example, they used the tactics of the "University Bill" of going. About £1,000 tutoring fees a year not including the cost to live. When I get out from University I will have a mortgage before I've even started. Then there's the fact that going to University might not even secure me a job. I'm going to have to start off with a minimum wage, and work my way up through a system so that I can finally get a wage that benefits me.

A dream I had as a little boy was that I wanted what my parents have got. And that is, each other and a family. But many families these days end up split up. WARNING: This is the part where I go on about how badly I want a girl friend! I would love the fact of having someone dependent on me. I am not the most outgoing lad that ever existed, and asking girls out is not my best forte. Hell I've only ever asked one girl to be my girlfriend, and that relationship lasted less than a week. But I think that effected me more than I want to believe. I have absolutely no confidence in asking a girl out what so ever now. This is backed up by the fact I've got nothing to talk about. Look at my list of hobbies up there? No I can't see it either. I'm not even a funny guy. I can't rely on the fact I can crack a joke here and there. I generally tend to work by bouncing off of what other people say, so I can't see a relationship working there, can you? This is why I get scared when I talk to girls that I like - I run out of things to say very fast, and can't hold a conversation going without a third person taking part.

I am a very self conscious person. I can't help that. No one can help me with that. I've always been like that, there's no way you can change it. I worry about what other people think of me, and what other people think about what I say or do. Different people have different visions of what is good/bad, cool/uncool. Another reason I wouldn't like to ask a girl out would be because I would worry what other people would think of me, and why I want to go out with that person. I know it's stupid because the person I'm going out with should be the only one I worry about, but I don't.

I hate embarrassment. I wish it would disappear. If it did, there would be no reason for me to worry. The reason I hate embarrassment is because of the difficult situations that it makes. I always try and change the atmosphere if someone feels embarrassed, because I hate what that person is feeling. It could also be another reason of why I wouldn't want to kiss etc. in public. Other people would watch and I would get embarrassed. A kiss is a personal thing, and other people watching kinda takes away the personal ness of it. The embarrassment would also be there if I were turned down by a girl. I would be petrified of that. I would also be very upset, because I would like that girl very much in order to pluck up the courage to ask her out in the first place. More stresses that I could do without. The consequence = I don't get anything. And haven't done since the dawn of time.

So really that's probably why I want to move away to a different University. It's not you guys. It's me. I want a relationship with another person very badly, and in order to do that, the girl doesn't already have to know me, and you guys can't be there for me to get embarrassed about asking her/doing relationship stuff in front of. So that's why I want to move away - I want a relationship, but I don't want to feel like you are breathing down my neck and looking at what I'm doing all the time. You are my childhood friends, and ones I will never forget, and I like to think I won't loose contact with. But University is all about meeting new people, and making new friends who will last you the rest of the course. I'm unlikely to feel as embarrassed in front of new people as I am you guys. Please don't take what I posted as a personal dig at you, but sometimes you stress me out. That and the fact I got all the above going on in my head. I love you guys... and I hope you know that.

If I do seem like I am in a mood or am being depressed, all I need is some space to re-work out in my head what is wrong. I will be all right. Thank you for caring, but my head seems to work out the present better than anyone else. All though, I'm going to have to teach it about working out the future. Oh yeah, and if I do say I don't want to go over someone's house, please don't be offended if I say "No", I'm probably tired, can't go because of school work or just don't want to go. It will be time that I need to spend alone. I don't know whether what I've said has made sense, or whether you've bothered to read to the end about my problems, but either way, this is how my brain wants to explain for my behaviour the last couple of months.
